The Amazon Deforestation

A data-driven story about the Amazon rainforest and Brazil

Project Motivation

Patriot Act with Hasan Minhaj is known for its comedic mixed-media take on current events. He often utilizes data-driven visualizations as his background to drive home facts surrounding current events from across the globe. In one of his recent episodes, he addressed the recent uptick in deforestation in the Amazon and its link to Brazil’s political corruption. The following webpage aims to explore this topic further through visualizations that are created using multiple databases, in an effort to tell the story behind the Amazon Deforestation that is currently occurring at an alarming rate.

The Amazon and Its Wonders

Known as the “Lungs of the Earth” protecting the amazon rainforest has been a hot topic for decades, but with the rising effect of climate change, deforestation has spread like a disease throughout the Earth’s lungs. (R1) The Amazon is located in South America and spans over 9 countries (as shown below).

Not only does the Amazon serve as earth’s cooling system, but it is also home to over 10% of the Earth’s species (R2) As a result of the rainforest, Brazil is ranked the number one most biodiverse areas on the planet. Below is a map showing the top ten most biodiverse countries in the world based on their BioDiversity Index number which is an index used to measure biodiversity in a region. (R3)

But It's Burning...

While deforestation began to drop post-2004, in the last five years it has begun to increase again. (R4) With the effects of climate change more relevant to the average person than ever, deforestation of the rain forest has also grown as a result of this climate change.

Dashboard available here

With the Amazon producing less water vapor and oxygen while simultaneously processing less CO2 as a result of its decreasing area, it is potentially exacerbating the effects of climate change. As one can see from the below, global temperatures are rising which is having a global impact such as sea ice melting which in turn is destroying species that are dependent on this area, such as polar bears, as well as raising the ocean levels. (R5) This has potentially catastrophic effects on the rest of the globe.

If we know climate change is negatively impacting our global ecosystems, and we know deforestation of rainforest isn’t helping, then why is the rainforest still being cut down?

Corruption

To answer this question, we have to turn to the beef processing industry. Roughly around the same time the deforestation rate of the Amazon began to increase after a record period of decrease, Brazil became the top cattle-producing country in the world. (Note: although India had the most “Cattle per Country” in the graphic below, the cattle are not often slaughtered for beef, as cows are sacred in that country.)

After doing some research, it is evident that this trend in the data is not a coincidence as many sections of the rainforest have been reportedly burned down to create land for cattle ranching. (R6) We decided to dive further into this connection using data visualizations. We can start by looking at the importance of cattle ranching to Brazil’s economy and major agribusiness companies.

Brazil has a huge export economy, exporting nearly $220 Billion (USD) to the world. And Brazil’s huge agribusiness industry,of which beef makes up a significant part, composes 23.5% or $51 Billion (USD) of those exports (as shown above).

JBS, one of the world’s largest meat processors, is headquartered in São Paulo, Brazil. In the last decade, JBS was exposed as having given $600 million in bribes to approximately 1,829 Brazilian government officials.(R7) In fact, Brazil’s current President, Jair Bolsonaro, has appointed senior members of his administration with histories of corruption and financial misconduct that have benefited JBS in the past. Bolsonaro himself has also received thousands of dollars in bribes from JBS as well, as shown below.

Based on this information, it can be concluded that the environmental dangers of deforestation are being overshadowed by the potential personal gain of corrupt government officials and the agribusiness-based profits for JBS.

In summary, as long as the incentives for agribusiness exist and government corruption continues, allowing large corporations to bypass environmental protection laws with minimal consequences, the rainforest will continue to burn. This will, in turn, continue to magnifying the effects of global warming. #savetherainforest #savethepolarbears
